A/B Testing - Udacity

Posted on 2019-02-21

##Overview

  1. What is A/B Testing

    1. Definiton
      A general methodology using online when you plan to test a new product or a feature

    2. Two groups

      1. Control Set: existing product / feature
      2. Experient Set: new product / version

    3. Notes

      1. What is your basic metrics
      2. How much time you need in order to let customer have a Plato experience

      3. Example - Udacity online finance course (website online product)

        1. User flow - customer funnel
          Visit homepage -> explore the website -> create the account -> complete the course / share on the blog
        2. Refining hypothesis
          • Initial hypothesis: change the “START NOW” button from orange to purple will increase how many students explore the course
          • Updating hypothesis: change the “START NOW” button from orange to purple will increase Click Through Probability of the button

        3. Which metric to use

          • Total number of courses completed -> not good, take too much time
          • Number of clicks on START NOW button -> not good, sensitive to absolute value who visit the page

          • Click Through Rate
            $$
            CTR = \frac{number\ of\ clicks}{number\ of\ page views}
            $$

          • Click Through Probability
            $$
            CTP = \frac{number\ of\ unique\ visitors\ who\ click}{number\ of\ unique\ visitors\ who\ visit}
            $$
            Generally, use rate to measure the usability of the website, and use probability to measure the total impact of the website (enter the second page in the funnel)

        4. Repeated measurement of Click Through Probability

    4. Math Foundation

      1. Binomial Distribution
        When N is big enough (N≥30), then it is close to the normal distribution
        $$
        Mean = p\\
Standard \ Deviation = \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{N}}
        $$
      2. Confidence Interval
        1. Two-tail Test Margin of Error (M)
          $$
          M=Z_{1-\frac{\alpha}{2}}SE=Z_{1-\frac{\alpha}{2}} \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{N}}
          $$
        2. One-tail Test Margin of Error (M)
          $$
          M=Z_{1-\alpha}SE=Z_{1-\alpha} \sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{N}}
          $$
      3. Hypothesis Testing / Inference
        1. Null Hypothesis / Baseline: there is no difference in CTP between our Control and Eexperiment
        2. Alternative Hypothesis: there is a difference / it is higher / it is lower
        3. In our experiment,
          Null Hypothesis $H_0$: $P_{control} - P_{experiment} = 0$
          Alternative Hypothesis $H_1$: $P_{control} - P_{experiment} ≠ 0$
          we calculate the $P(P_{control} - P_{experiment} = 0\ |\ H_0)$ and reject $H_0$ if the probability is smaller than our threshold $\alpha$, usually $\alpha = 0.05$
      4. Compare Two Samples
        $$
        P'_{pool} = \frac{X_{count}+X_{experiment}}{N_{count}+N_{experiment}}\\
Standard \ Deviation= \sqrt{P'_{pool}*(1-P'_{pool})*(\frac{1}{N_{count}}+\frac{1}{N_{experiment}})}
        $$
      5. How many page views (sample size)
        $$
        \alpha = P(reject\ null\ |\ null\ is\ true)\\
\beta =  P(fail\ to\ reject\ null\ |\ null\ is\ false)\\
sensitivity = 1 - \beta\ (often\ 80\%)
        $$
        • Small Sample: $\alpha$ is low, $\beta$ is high
        • Large Sample: $\alpha$ is same, $\beta$ is lower
        • How to calculate the page views(sample size)
          1. built-in library
          2. look up answer in a table
          3. Use online calculator - Sample Size Calculator
            (enter: practical significance, $1-\beta$, $\alpha$)
      6. Analyze the Result

Metrics for Experiments

  1. How to Define A Metrics
    1. Two main Use Cases
      1. Invariant Tracking / Sanity Checking Metrics 完整性检查指标
        These metrics wont change in the experiment group and control group. (i.e. populaiton, sample size, distribution)
      2. Evaluation Metrics 评价指标
        1. Overall Business Metrics (i.e. profit, market share, how many users)
        2. More Detailed Metrics (i.e. measure user experience of a specific product)
    2. High-Level Concept for Metrics
      • Lets foucs on an example, and to see how it work.
        1. Firstly, we start from our business objective.
          • Audacity cares about helping students find the job in finance.
          • Financial substainability
        2. Make the customer funnel more detailed
          Fewer and fewer users do down into the funnel. Each stage is a metric. Absolute Number of users who reach that point. Rates or Probabilities want to increase rate of progression.
          • Homepage visits
          • Exploring the site
            • Users who view course list
            • Users who view course details
          • Create an account
            • Users who enroll in a course
            • Users who finish Lesson1, Lesson2, etc
            • Users who sign up for coaching at various level
          • Completing a course
            • Users who enroll in a second course
            • Users who get a job
    3. Choosing Metrics
      • Lets foucs on an example, and to see how it work.
        1. Objective1: Update a description on a course list page
          • Primary Metrics: Probability of progressing from course list page to course page
          • Secondary Metrics: Continued progression down the funnel
        2. Objective2: Increase size of “Start Now” button
          • Primary Metrics: Click-Through-Rate on “Start Now” button
        3. Objective3: Explain benefits of paid service
          • Primary Metrics: Probability that enrolled students pay for coaching
          • Secondary Metrics: User retention among each time period
    4. Dealing with Difficult Metrics
      1. Reason of difficulties
        1. Don’t have access to the data
        2. Takes too long to collect
      2. Techniques of defining metrics (pdf)
        1. Impact
          • Generating new metrics
          • Validating possible metrics
        2. Techniques
          1. External Data
          2. Own Data
    5. Gathering Additional Data
      1. User Experience Research (UER)
        • Better to get detailed / deep infomation
        • Can use special equipment
      2. Focused Groups 焦点小组访谈
        • More people
        • Cannot get detailed infomation with each of them
      3. Surveys
        • Useful for metrics that you could not directly measure
        • Cannot directly compare with other results
